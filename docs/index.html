Humanoid Backflip

Vihaan Shah & Shriyash Bajaj

Abstract. Control stack for executing a humanoid backflip using optimization-based planning and phase-structured whole-body control. A kino-dynamic trajectory optimizer generates a feasible acrobatic motion that jointly searches over centroidal momentum and limb kinematics, providing a consistency framework that can extend naturally to other aerial maneuvers. The offline motion is computed using a non-convex trajectory optimization and then tracked online using a whole-body impulse controller during push-off and landing, with a reduced-order MPC regulating landing contact wrench, achieving a fully analytical backflip without learning, motion capture, or heuristic references.
